CAUTI & ABUTI Definitions (NHSN 2026)

SUTI 1a (Catheter-Associated): Indwelling catheter in place for >2 calendar days. Symptom (Fever/Pain) + Culture ≥10^5 CFU/ml. SUTI 1b (Catheter-Associated): Catheter removed day of or before event. Symptom + Culture ≥10^5 CFU/ml. SUTI 2 (Pediatric - With or Without Catheter): Patient ≤1 yr. Fever/Apnea/Brady/Lethargy/Vomiting + Culture ≥10^5 CFU/ml. ABUTI (With or Without Catheter): No symptoms + Culture ≥10^5 CFU/ml + Matching blood culture organism.

CLABSI (LCBI) Definitions

LCBI 1: Any age. Recognized pathogen from 1+ blood cultures. LCBI 2: >1 yr. Fever/Chills/Hypotension + 2 matching commensals. LCBI 3: ≤1 yr. Fever/Hypothermia/Apnea/Brady + 2 matching commensals.

SSI Definitions & Procedures

Superficial: Skin/SubQ only. Purulent drainage or signs + physician diagnosis. Deep: Fascia/Muscle. Purulent drainage or dehiscence + fever. Organ/Space: Deeper than muscle. Drainage from organ drain or abscess on imaging. Procedures: • 30-Day: APPY, CHOL, COLO, HYST, OVRY, SB, THOR. • 90-Day: CARD, CBGB, CRAN, FUSN, HPRO, KPRO, VSHN.

VAE & pedVAE Definitions

VAC: Stability (2 days) then PEEP rise ≥3 or FiO2 rise ≥0.20 (2 days). pedVAE: Stability (2 days) then MAP rise ≥4 or FiO2 rise ≥0.20 (2 days). PVAP: iVAC criteria met + Positive culture (ETA ≥10^5, BAL ≥10^4).
