CLMMRH Physician Reference Manual

This manual provides the clinical rationale for HAI surveillance based on NHSN protocols.

Laboratory-Confirmed BSI (LCBI)

Note: A Central Line must be in place > 2 days. If removed, the Date of Event (DOE) must be the day of or day after removal.

LCBI 1 (Pathogen Path)

Isolated recognized pathogen (e.g., Staphylococcus aureus, Pseudomonas aeruginosa, E. coli, Klebsiella) from 1 or more blood cultures. Symptoms are not required for this classification.

LCBI 2 & 3 (Commensal Path)

Common commensal (e.g., Coagulase-negative Staphylococci, Micrococcus, Corynebacterium) isolated from 2 or more blood cultures collected on separate occasions + Fever (>38.0C) or Chills. LCBI 3 is the specific neonatal variant for patients ≤1 year.

Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)

SUTI 1a / 1b (Symptomatic)

Requires culture of ≥10^5 CFU/ml with no more than 2 species of organisms + at least one localized symptom (Fever, Suprapubic tenderness, CVA pain). SUTI 2 is utilized for neonatal patients where localized pain cannot be assessed.

ABUTI (Asymptomatic Bacteremic UTI)

Patient has no clinical symptoms but meets the culture threshold (≥10^5) AND has a matching organism in a blood culture. This effectively identifies the urinary tract as the source of a BSI.

Surgical Site Infection (SSI)

Anatomical Classification

  • Superficial: Skin and subcutaneous tissue only.
  • Deep: Fascial and muscle layers.
  • Organ/Space: Any part of the body deeper than the fascial layer that was opened or manipulated.

Surveillance Windows

30 days for general procedures. 90 days for procedures involving implants, cardiovascular surgery, or neurosurgical interventions.

Ventilator-Associated Events (VAE)

Stability Requirement: VAE cannot be triggered unless there is at least 2 days of "stability" or "improvement" in oxygenation parameters immediately preceding the deterioration.

VAC (Ventilator-Associated Condition)

Purely based on oxygenation deterioration. A sustained increase in daily minimum PEEP (≥3 cmH2O) or FiO2 (≥0.20) for ≥2 days.

IVAC (Infection-related VAC)

VAC + Temperature (>38 or <36) or WBC (≥12,000 or ≤4,000) AND administration of a new antimicrobial agent for ≥4 days.

PVAP (Possible VAP)

IVAC + Positive respiratory culture (protected specimen brush ≥10^3, BAL ≥10^4) or histopathology.
