CAUTI 2026 Definitions

SUTI 1a: Patient has an indwelling urinary catheter in place for >2 calendar days on the date of event (Day 1 = catheter insertion) AND has at least one: fever (>38.0°C), suprapubic tenderness, costovertebral angle pain, or urgency/frequency/dysuria AND culture with no more than two species, at least one is ≥10^5 CFU/ml. SUTI 1b: Patient has an indwelling urinary catheter that was removed the day of or the day before the date of event AND at least one: fever (>38.0°C), suprapubic tenderness, costovertebral angle pain, or urgency/frequency/dysuria AND culture with no more than two species, at least one is ≥10^5 CFU/ml. SUTI 2 (Pediatric): Patient ≤ 1 year of age (with or without catheter) has at least one of the following: fever (>38.0°C), hypothermia (<36.0°C), apnea, bradycardia, lethargy, or vomiting AND urine culture with no more than two species, at least one is ≥10^5 CFU/ml. ABUTI (Asymptomatic Bacteremic UTI): Patient has no localized urinary signs/symptoms (e.g., no fever, no urgency) but has a urine culture with no more than two species, at least one is ≥10^5 CFU/ml, AND a positive blood culture with at least one matching organism.

CLABSI (LCBI) Definitions

LCBI 1: Patient of any age has a recognized pathogen (not a common commensal) identified from one or more blood specimens. LCBI 2: Patient > 1 year of age has at least one: fever (>38.0°C), chills, or hypotension AND matching common commensals (e.g., CoNS) identified from two or more blood specimens collected on separate occasions (within the GAP). LCBI 3: Patient ≤ 1 year of age has at least one: fever (>38.0°C), hypothermia (<36.0°C), apnea, or bradycardia AND matching common commensals identified from two or more blood specimens collected on separate occasions.

SSI Criteria & Procedures

Superficial Incisional: Involves only skin and subcutaneous tissue. Requires purulent drainage, culture identification, or localized signs (pain, swelling, redness) plus physician diagnosis. Deep Incisional: Involves fascia and muscle layers. Requires purulent drainage, spontaneous dehiscence with fever, or an abscess/evidence of infection on imaging/re-op. Organ/Space: Involves any part of the body deeper than the fascial/muscle layers that was opened or manipulated. Requires purulent drainage from a drain in the organ/space or abscess. Surveillance Windows: • 30-Day: APPY, CHOL, COLO, HYST, OVRY, SB, THOR. • 90-Day: CARD, CBGB, CBGC, CRAN, FUSN, HPRO, KPRO, PVBY, RFN, VSHN.

VAE & pedVAE Definitions

VAC: Patient has baseline stability (≥2 days) followed by an increase in daily minimum PEEP (≥3 cmH2O) or FiO2 (≥0.20) sustained for ≥2 days. iVAC: Meets VAC criteria AND has temperature (>38°C or <36°C) or WBC (≥12k or ≤4k) AND a new antimicrobial agent is started and continued for ≥4 days. PVAP (Possible VAP): Meets iVAC criteria AND has positive respiratory culture (ETA ≥10^5, BAL ≥10^4) OR positive histopathology. pedVAE: Specifically for <18 years. Includes Mean Airway Pressure (MAP) rise of ≥4 cmH2O as a primary indicator of respiratory deterioration alongside PEEP and FiO2.
