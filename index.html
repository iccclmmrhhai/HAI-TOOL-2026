Bloodstream Infection (BSI/CLABSI)

LCBI 1: Recognized pathogen identified from 1+ blood cultures. [cite: 2222] LCBI 2 (Patients > 1 yr): Fever (>38.0°C), chills, or hypotension AND matching common commensals identified from 2+ blood cultures drawn on separate occasions. [cite: 2223] LCBI 3 (Patients ≤ 1 yr): Fever, hypothermia, apnea, or bradycardia AND matching common commensals identified from 2+ blood cultures drawn on separate occasions. [cite: 2224]

Urinary Tract Infection (UTI/CAUTI)

SUTI 1a (Catheter-Associated): Patient has an indwelling catheter in place for >2 days AND has symptoms (fever, suprapubic tenderness, CVA pain/tenderness) AND urine culture ≥10^5 CFU/ml. SUTI 1b (Catheter-Associated): Catheter removed on day of or day before event AND has symptoms AND culture ≥10^5 CFU/ml. SUTI 2 (Pediatric ≤ 1 yr): Fever, apnea, bradycardia, lethargy, or vomiting AND culture ≥10^5 CFU/ml. *May meet with or without catheter.* ABUTI (Asymptomatic Bacteremic UTI): No SUTI symptoms AND culture ≥10^5 CFU/ml AND positive blood culture with at least one matching bacteria. *May meet with or without catheter.*

Surgical Site Infection (SSI)

Superficial Incisional: Involves only skin and subcutaneous tissue. Requires purulent drainage or symptoms + physician diagnosis. [cite: 100] Deep Incisional: Involves fascia and muscle. Requires purulent drainage OR spontaneous dehiscence + fever/pain OR abscess found on exam/imaging. Organ/Space: Involves any part of the body deeper than fascia/muscle opened or manipulated during procedure. Requires purulent drainage from a drain in the organ/space OR positive culture from organ/space tissue OR abscess found on exam/imaging. [cite: 109, 110, 111, 112] Surveillance Windows: • 30-Day Window: APPY, CHOL, COLO, HYST, OVRY, SB, THOR. • 90-Day Window: CARD, CBGB, CRAN, FUSN, HPRO, KPRO, VSHN.

Ventilator-Associated Event (VAE)

VAC (Ventilator-Associated Condition): ≥2 days of stability/improvement followed by ≥2 days of worsening oxygenation (Daily min PEEP increase ≥3 cmH2O OR FiO2 increase ≥0.20). [cite: 879, 963] IVAC (Infection-related): VAC criteria met AND temperature >38.0°C or <36.0°C OR WBC ≥12,000 or ≤4,000 AND a new antimicrobial agent is started and continued for ≥4 days. [cite: 1107] PVAP (Possible VAP): IVAC criteria met AND positive culture of respiratory tract specimen (ETA/Sputum ≥10^5, BAL ≥10^4) OR lung histopathology/diagnostic tests for specific pathogens. [cite: 943, 960]
