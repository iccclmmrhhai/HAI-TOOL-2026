NHSN Surveillance Definitions (2026)

CLABSI (Bloodstream)

LCBI 1: 1+ Recognized Pathogen (e.g., S. aureus, E. coli, Klebsiella) in blood culture. No symptoms required

LCBI 2: 2+ Common Commensals (e.g., CoNS) + Adult symptoms (Fever/Chills).

LCBI 3: 2+ Common Commensals + Infant symptoms (Apnea/Bradycardia).

CAUTI (Urinary)

SUTI 1a: Foley in place > 2 days on Date of Event.

SUTI 1b: Foley removed day before or day of event.

SUTI 2: Pediatric criteria for patients ≤ 1 year.

ABUTI: Asymptomatic Bacteremic UTI. Culture ≥10^5 matches blood culture organism with no symptoms.

SSI (Surgical)

Superficial: Skin and subcutaneous tissue only. Window is always 30 days.

Deep: Fascia and muscle layers. Window can be 30 or 90 days.

Organ/Space: Any part of the body deeper than fascia opened or manipulated.

VAE (Ventilator)

VAC: Respiratory decline only (PEEP/FiO2).

IVAC: VAC + General signs of infection (Temp/WBC) + New Antibiotics for ≥4 days.

PVAP: IVAC + Lab evidence (Positive sputum/BAL culture).
