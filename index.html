CLABSI Definitions

LCBI 3: Occurs in neonates ($\le$ 1 year). Clinical signs must include at least one of: fever, hypothermia, apnea, or bradycardia + 2 matching commensal cultures.

CAUTI Nuances

ABUTI: Patient has no symptoms, but same organism is grown from urine ($\ge 10^5$) and blood.

Standardized Infection Ratio (SIR)

The SIR is our primary metric. $SIR = \frac{Observed}{Predicted}$. A SIR < 1.0 indicates our IPC protocols at CLMMRH are outperforming benchmarks.

Device Utilization (DU)

Tracking DU ratios for ventilators and central lines helps identify wards where device dependency—and thus infection risk—is highest.
