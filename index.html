CLMMRH Master Clinical Manual

Strict surveillance definitions for physicians and IPC nurses.

I. Bloodstream Infections (BSI/CLABSI)

Criteria for LCBI 1

Recognized pathogen isolated from 1+ blood cultures. Pathogens include: S. aureus, Enterococcus spp., E. coli, Pseudomonas spp., Klebsiella spp., Candida spp.

Criteria for LCBI 2 & 3

Requires 2+ matching cultures of Common Commensals (e.g., CoNS, Micrococcus, Diphtheroids, Bacillus spp.) PLUS clinical symptoms (Fever >38.0°C, Chills, or Hypotension). For LCBI 3 (Infants), include Apnea or Bradycardia.

II. Urinary Tract Infections (UTI/CAUTI)

SUTI 1a (Catheter-Associated)

Catheter in place >2 days on Date of Event. Culture: ≥10^5 CFU/ml (max 2 species). Symptoms: Fever, suprapubic pain, or CVA tenderness.

ABUTI

No localized symptoms, but meets culture threshold AND blood culture organism matches urine isolate.

III. Surgical Site Infections (SSI)

Superficial Incisional

Involves only skin or subcutaneous tissue. Must occur within 30 days of procedure.

Deep Incisional

Involves deep soft tissues (fascia and muscle). Window: 30 or 90 days. Must have purulent drainage OR abscess OR spontaneous dehisence.

Organ/Space

Involves any part of the body deeper than the fascial/muscle layers that was opened or manipulated. Requires imaging evidence or re-operation findings.

IV. Ventilator-Associated Events (VAE)

The "Stability" Rule: A VAE cannot be classified if the patient has not had a minimum of 2 calendar days of stability or improvement in PEEP or FiO2 prior to the deterioration.

VAC

Deterioration must be sustained for ≥2 days. Daily min PEEP increase ≥3 OR daily min FiO2 increase ≥0.20.

IVAC

The "Infectious" component. Requires VAC criteria plus a new antimicrobial agent started and continued for ≥4 days, alongside inflammatory markers (WBC/Temp).

PVAP

The "Microbiological" component. Requires IVAC criteria plus quantitative/semi-quantitative respiratory cultures or histopathology.
