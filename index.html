I. BSI / CLABSI DEFINITIONS

LCBI 1: Patient of any age has at least one blood culture with a recognized pathogen (e.g., S. aureus, E. coli, Klebsiella, Pseudomonas, Candida).

LCBI 2: Patient > 1 year of age has at least one of the following: fever (>38.0°C), chills, or hypotension AND matching common commensals (e.g., CoNS) are cultured from two or more blood cultures collected on separate occasions.

LCBI 3: Patient ≤ 1 year of age has at least one of the following: fever (>38.0°C core), hypothermia (<36.0°C core), apnea, or bradycardia AND matching common commensals are cultured from two or more blood cultures collected on separate occasions.

II. UTI / CAUTI DEFINITIONS

SUTI 1a: Patient must have an indwelling urinary catheter in place for >2 calendar days on the date of event AND at least one of: fever (>38.0°C), suprapubic tenderness, costovertebral angle pain, or urinary urgency/frequency/dysuria.

ABUTI: Patient has no localized urinary signs/symptoms but has a urine culture with no more than two species of organisms, at least one of which is ≥10^5 CFU/ml, AND a positive blood culture with at least one matching organism.

IV. VAE (VENTILATOR-ASSOCIATED EVENTS)

VAC (Ventilator-Associated Condition): A baseline period of stability or improvement (defined as ≥2 calendar days of stable or decreasing daily minimum PEEP or FiO2) followed by a period of worsening oxygenation (defined as ≥2 calendar days of increased daily minimum PEEP of ≥3 cmH2O or FiO2 of ≥0.20).

IVAC (Infection-related VAC): On or after calendar day 3 of mechanical ventilation and within 2 days before or after the onset of VAC, the patient meets: 1) Temp >38.0°C or <36.0°C OR WBC ≥12,000 or ≤4,000 cells/mm3; AND 2) A new antimicrobial agent is started and continued for ≥4 calendar days.
