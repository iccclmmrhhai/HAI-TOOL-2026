Detailed NHSN 2026 Definitions

Urinary Tract (SUTI 1b & 2)

SUTI 1b (Non-CAUTI or Post-Removal): Patient has one localized symptom AND a culture ≥10^5 CFU/ml, but the catheter was removed >1 day prior to the date of event OR was never in place.

SUTI 2 (Pediatric): Patient ≤ 1 year of age (with or without catheter) has at least one: Fever (>38.0°C), hypothermia (<36.0°C), apnea, bradycardia, dysuria, lethargy, or vomiting AND a urine culture ≥10^5 CFU/ml.

Ventilator Events (PVAP & pedVAE)

PVAP (Possible VAP): Meets IVAC criteria PLUS one of the following:

  • Positive culture from endotracheal aspirate (≥10^5 CFU/ml), BAL (≥10^4 CFU/ml), or Lung Tissue (≥10^4 CFU/g).
  • Purulent respiratory secretions (≥25 neutrophils/lpf and ≤10 squamous cells/lpf).
  • Positive lung histopathology or pleural fluid culture matching respiratory specimen.

pedVAE (Pediatric VAE): Specifically for patients < 18 years. Logic follows VAC, but includes Mean Airway Pressure (MAP) rise of ≥4 cmH2O as a deterioration metric alongside PEEP and FiO2.

BSI (CLABSI)

LCBI 1: 1+ Pathogen culture. LCBI 2/3: 2+ Commensal cultures + Clinical Symptoms.
