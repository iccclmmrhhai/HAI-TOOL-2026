2026 NHSN/CDC Clinical Reference

SUTI 1b (Non-CAUTI)

Criteria for symptomatic UTI where an indwelling catheter was not in place for >2 days at the time of event, or was removed more than 2 calendar days prior. Requires:

  • At least one: Fever (>38.0°C), urgency, frequency, dysuria, suprapubic tenderness, or CVA pain.
  • Urine culture: ≥10^5 CFU/ml (max 2 species).

SUTI 2 (Pediatric ≤1 Year)

Specific for neonatal/infant populations (with or without catheter):

  • At least one: Fever (>38.0°C core), hypothermia (<36.0°C core), apnea, bradycardia, lethargy, or vomiting.
  • Urine culture: ≥10^5 CFU/ml (max 2 species).

PVAP (Possible VAP)

Requires IVAC criteria plus one of the following microbiological thresholds:

  • Endotracheal aspirate: ≥10^5 CFU/ml (semi-quant: 3+ or 4+).
  • BAL: ≥10^4 CFU/ml.
  • Protected Specimen Brush: ≥10^3 CFU/ml.
  • Lung Tissue: ≥10^4 CFU/g.

pedVAE (Pediatric VAE)

For patients <18 years. Includes Mean Airway Pressure (MAP) rise as a primary metric for VAC:

  • VAC: 2 days stability followed by 2 days of: PEEP rise ≥3, FiO2 rise ≥0.20, OR Daily min MAP rise ≥4 cmH2O.
